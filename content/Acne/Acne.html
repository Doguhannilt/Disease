Acne Guide

What is Acne?

Acne is a common skin condition that occurs when hair follicles become clogged with oil and dead skin cells. It often leads to the development of pimples, blackheads, and whiteheads on the face, chest, and back.

Acne Types

Acne can manifest in various forms, such as:

  • Whiteheads
  • Blackheads
  • Papules
  • Pustules
  • Cysts

Causes and Triggers

Acne can be caused by a combination of factors, including excess oil production, clogged pores, bacteria, and inflammation. Hormonal changes, stress, and certain medications can also trigger or worsen acne.

Treatment and Prevention

Treatment for acne may include topical creams, oral medications, and lifestyle changes. Keeping the skin clean, avoiding squeezing pimples, and maintaining a balanced diet can help prevent acne breakouts.

Ölüm Riski (Risk of Severe Acne)

Acne, in most cases, does not pose a severe risk to life. However, severe and untreated acne can lead to permanent scarring and emotional distress. Early intervention and proper care can help manage and prevent complications.
