Cervical Spondylosis Information

What is Cervical Spondylosis?

Cervical Spondylosis is a common degenerative condition that affects the cervical spine (neck region). It is caused by the wear and tear of the cartilage and bones in the neck over time. As we age, the discs between the vertebrae lose moisture and become less flexible, leading to cervical spondylosis.

Symptoms of Cervical Spondylosis

Cervical spondylosis can cause a range of symptoms, including:

  • Neck pain and stiffness
  • Headaches
  • Shoulder and arm pain
  • Numbness or weakness in the arms or hands
  • Difficulty in walking or maintaining balance
  • Loss of bladder or bowel control (in severe cases)

Causes and Risk Factors

The primary cause of cervical spondylosis is aging and the natural wear and tear of the spine. However, certain factors may increase the risk of developing the condition, including:

  • Smoking
  • Occupations involving repetitive neck movements
  • Previous neck injuries
  • Genetic predisposition
  • Obesity

Diagnosis and Treatment

If you experience persistent neck pain or other symptoms, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional. The diagnosis may involve physical examination, imaging tests (X-rays, MRI), and neurological evaluation.

Treatment options for cervical spondylosis include:

  • Physical therapy and exercises
  • Medications for pain and inflammation
  • Use of neck braces or collars
  • Injections for pain relief
  • Surgery (in severe cases or when other treatments don't work)
