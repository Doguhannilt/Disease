Chickenpox Information

What is Chickenpox?

Chickenpox, also known as Varicella, is a highly contagious viral infection caused by the Varicella-Zoster virus. It is characterized by an itchy rash that usually starts on the face and trunk, and then spreads throughout the body. Chickenpox is common in children but can affect people of all ages.

Symptoms of Chickenpox

Common symptoms of Chickenpox include:

  • Red, itchy rash
  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Fatigue
  • Loss of appetite
  • Fluid-filled blisters

Complications of Chickenpox

While most cases of Chickenpox are mild, some individuals may experience complications, especially in certain high-risk groups. Common complications include bacterial skin infections, pneumonia, and encephalitis (inflammation of the brain).
