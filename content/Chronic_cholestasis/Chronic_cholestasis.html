Chronic Cholecystitis Information

What is Chronic Cholecystitis?

Chronic Cholecystitis is a medical condition characterized by inflammation of the gallbladder. It typically occurs when there is a prolonged presence of gallstones in the gallbladder, causing irritation and inflammation of its lining. Chronic Cholecystitis may lead to various digestive problems and discomfort.

Symptoms of Chronic Cholecystitis

Common symptoms of Chronic Cholecystitis include:

  • Abdominal pain, particularly in the upper right or center of the abdomen
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Indigestion and bloating
  • Gas and burping
  • Feeling of fullness after meals
  • Occasional fever and chills

Causes and Risk Factors

Chronic Cholecystitis is primarily caused by gallstones (cholelithiasis) that block the bile ducts and lead to inflammation. Other risk factors include:

  • Obesity and a high-fat diet
  • Rapid weight loss or fasting
  • Family history of gallbladder problems
  • Diabetes
  • Pregnancy (due to hormonal changes)

Diagnosis and Treatment

A healthcare professional can diagnose Chronic Cholecystitis through physical examination, imaging tests (ultrasound, CT scan), and blood tests. Treatment options may include:

  • Dietary changes to reduce fat intake
  • Medications to dissolve gallstones or relieve symptoms
  • Surgery to remove the gallbladder (cholecystectomy) in severe cases
