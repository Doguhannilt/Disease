Common Cold Information

What is the Common Cold?

The common cold is a viral infection primarily caused by different types of rhinoviruses. It affects the upper respiratory system, including the nose and throat. The common cold is highly contagious and can spread through droplets when an infected person coughs or sneezes.

Symptoms of the Common Cold

Common symptoms of the common cold include:

  • Runny or stuffy nose
  • Sneezing
  • Cough
  • Sore throat
  • Mild headache
  • Low-grade fever (rare)

Prevention and Treatment

The common cold can be prevented by practicing good hygiene, such as washing hands frequently, avoiding close contact with sick individuals, and covering the mouth and nose while coughing or sneezing. Treatment options include rest, staying hydrated, over-the-counter medications for symptom relief (e.g., decongestants, pain relievers), and home remedies like warm fluids and throat lozenges.
