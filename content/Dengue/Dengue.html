Dengue Information

What is Dengue?

Dengue is a mosquito-borne viral infection caused by the dengue virus. It is primarily transmitted through the bites of infected Aedes mosquitoes. Dengue is prevalent in tropical and subtropical regions and can cause mild to severe flu-like symptoms.

Symptoms of Dengue

Common symptoms of Dengue include:

  • Sudden high fever
  • Severe headaches
  • Pain behind the eyes
  • Joint and muscle pain
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Skin rash (petechial rash)

Complications and Severity

In some cases, Dengue can progress to severe Dengue, also known as Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever (DHF) or Dengue Shock Syndrome (DSS). Severe Dengue can lead to bleeding, organ failure, and even death. Early detection and medical care are crucial in preventing severe complications.

Prevention and Treatment

Preventive measures for Dengue include:

  • Using mosquito repellent
  • Wearing protective clothing
  • Eliminating mosquito breeding sites
  • Using mosquito nets

There is no specific antiviral treatment for Dengue. Symptomatic relief and supportive care are provided to manage fever, pain, and dehydration. In severe cases, hospitalization and intensive medical care may be required.
