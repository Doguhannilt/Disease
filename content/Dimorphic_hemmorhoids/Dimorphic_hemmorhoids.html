Hemorrhoids Information

What are Hemorrhoids?

Hemorrhoids, also known as piles, are swollen and inflamed veins in the rectum and anus. They can be internal (inside the rectum) or external (under the skin around the anus). Hemorrhoids are a common condition and can be caused by increased pressure on the veins in the lower rectum.

Symptoms of Hemorrhoids

Common symptoms of hemorrhoids include:

  • Painless bleeding during bowel movements
  • Itching or irritation in the anal area
  • Pain or discomfort, especially during sitting
  • A lump or swelling around the anus
  • Leakage of feces

Causes and Risk Factors

Hemorrhoids can be caused by various factors, including:

  • Straining during bowel movements
  • Chronic constipation or diarrhea
  • Pregnancy
  • Obesity
  • Prolonged sitting or standing
  • Low-fiber diet

Treatment and Prevention

Mild cases of hemorrhoids can often be managed with home remedies, such as:

  • Increasing fiber intake to soften stools
  • Drinking plenty of water
  • Using over-the-counter creams or ointments
  • Taking warm baths (sitz baths)

Severe or persistent hemorrhoids may require medical treatment, such as rubber band ligation, sclerotherapy, or surgical removal.
