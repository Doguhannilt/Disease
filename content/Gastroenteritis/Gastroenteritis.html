Gastroenteritis Information

What is Gastroenteritis?

Gastroenteritis, often referred to as the stomach flu, is an inflammation of the stomach and intestines. It is commonly caused by viral or bacterial infections and can lead to symptoms such as diarrhea, vomiting, abdominal cramps, and sometimes fever. Gastroenteritis can be highly contagious and spreads through contaminated food, water, or contact with infected individuals.

Symptoms of Gastroenteritis

Common symptoms of gastroenteritis include:

  • Diarrhea
  • Vomiting
  • Abdominal cramps
  • Nausea
  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Dehydration

Causes and Risk Factors

Gastroenteritis can be caused by various factors, including:

  • Viral infections (e.g., norovirus, rotavirus)
  • Bacterial infections (e.g., E. coli, Salmonella)
  • Parasitic infections (e.g., Giardia)
  • Contaminated food or water
  • Close contact with infected individuals
  • Poor hygiene practices

Treatment and Prevention

Treatment for gastroenteritis focuses on managing symptoms and preventing dehydration. It may include rest, oral rehydration solutions, and avoiding certain foods. Antibiotics are generally not used for viral gastroenteritis but may be prescribed for bacterial causes. Proper hygiene practices, such as frequent handwashing, can help prevent the spread of gastroenteritis.
