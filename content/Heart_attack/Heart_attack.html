Heart Attack Information

What is a Heart Attack?

A heart attack, also known as a myocardial infarction (MI), occurs when there is a sudden blockage of blood flow to the heart muscle. It is often caused by the formation of a blood clot in a coronary artery, which supplies blood to the heart. Without adequate blood flow, the heart muscle can be damaged, leading to serious complications.

Symptoms of a Heart Attack

Common symptoms of a heart attack may include:

  • Chest pain or discomfort (often described as pressure, squeezing, or fullness)
  • Pain or discomfort in the arms, back, neck, jaw, or stomach
  • Shortness of breath
  • Cold sweat
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Lightheadedness or fainting

Causes and Risk Factors

The primary cause of a heart attack is the buildup of fatty deposits (plaques) in the coronary arteries, a condition known as atherosclerosis. Various risk factors can increase the likelihood of developing a heart attack, including:

  • High blood pressure (hypertension)
  • High cholesterol levels
  • Smoking or exposure to secondhand smoke
  • Diabetes
  • Obesity
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Family history of heart disease

Treatment and Prevention

Immediate treatment for a heart attack involves seeking emergency medical care. Early intervention can help minimize heart muscle damage and improve outcomes. Treatments may include medications, such as blood thinners and clot-busting drugs, and medical procedures, such as angioplasty and stent placement.

Preventive measures to reduce the risk of heart attacks include adopting a heart-healthy lifestyle, managing risk factors, eating a balanced diet, engaging in regular physical activity, and avoiding tobacco products.
