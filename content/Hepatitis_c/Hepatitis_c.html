Hepatitis C Information

What is Hepatitis C?

Hepatitis C is a viral infection that primarily affects the liver. It is caused by the hepatitis C virus (HCV) and can lead to chronic liver disease, cirrhosis, and liver cancer if left untreated. Hepatitis C is usually spread through contact with infected blood, but it can also be transmitted through sexual contact and from mother to child during childbirth.

Symptoms of Hepatitis C

Hepatitis C often presents as a silent infection, and many individuals may not experience noticeable symptoms in the early stages. However, as the disease progresses, symptoms may include:

  • Fatigue
  • Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes)
  • Abdominal pain or discomfort
  • Loss of appetite
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Dark urine
  • Joint pain

Causes and Risk Factors

Hepatitis C is primarily transmitted through:

  • Injection drug use and sharing of needles
  • Receiving contaminated blood or blood products (prior to blood screening)
  • Unprotected sexual contact with an infected person (less common)
  • From mother to child during childbirth (less common)

Treatment and Prevention

Advances in medical treatment have made it possible to cure hepatitis C in many cases. Antiviral medications are now available that can effectively clear the virus from the body and prevent further liver damage. Early diagnosis and treatment are crucial to prevent complications.

Prevention of hepatitis C involves avoiding exposure to infected blood and practicing safe behaviors, such as not sharing needles or personal items that may be contaminated with blood.
