Hepatitis E Information

What is Hepatitis E?

Hepatitis E is a viral infection that primarily affects the liver. It is caused by the hepatitis E virus (HEV). While most cases of hepatitis E are self-limiting and resolve on their own, the infection can be severe and even life-threatening, especially in pregnant women and individuals with pre-existing liver disease.

Symptoms of Hepatitis E

Symptoms of hepatitis E may include:

  • Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes)
  • Fatigue
  • Loss of appetite
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Abdominal pain or discomfort
  • Fever
  • Joint pain

Causes and Risk Factors

Hepatitis E is primarily transmitted through:

  • Consumption of contaminated water
  • Consumption of undercooked or raw pork, deer, or wild boar meat
  • Person-to-person contact in areas with poor sanitation

Treatment and Prevention

Most cases of hepatitis E do not require specific treatment and resolve on their own. Supportive care is provided to manage symptoms and ensure adequate hydration. In severe cases, hospitalization may be necessary.

Prevention of hepatitis E involves practicing good hygiene, including safe food preparation and avoiding the consumption of contaminated water and undercooked or raw meat.
