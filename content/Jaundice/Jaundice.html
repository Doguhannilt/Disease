Jaundice Information

What is Jaundice?

Jaundice is a medical condition characterized by the yellowing of the skin, eyes, and mucous membranes due to the buildup of bilirubin in the blood. Bilirubin is a yellow pigment produced during the breakdown of red blood cells. Jaundice can indicate an underlying liver, gallbladder, or blood disorder.

Symptoms of Jaundice

Common symptoms of jaundice include:

  • Yellowing of the skin and eyes
  • Dark-colored urine
  • Pale-colored stools
  • Itchy skin
  • Abdominal pain or swelling
  • Fever
  • Fatigue

Causes and Risk Factors

Jaundice can be caused by various factors, including:

  • Liver diseases (hepatitis, cirrhosis)
  • Gallbladder disorders (gallstones, cholecystitis)
  • Blood disorders (hemolytic anemia)
  • Pancreatic cancer

Treatment and Management

Treatment for jaundice depends on the underlying cause. In some cases, no specific treatment is required as the condition resolves on its own. For certain liver or gallbladder disorders, medications or surgical interventions may be necessary. Managing the underlying condition is essential to treat jaundice effectively.
