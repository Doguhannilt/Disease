Osteoarthritis Information

What is Osteoarthritis?

Osteoarthritis is a degenerative joint disease and the most common form of arthritis. It occurs when the protective cartilage that cushions the ends of bones wears down over time, leading to joint pain and stiffness. Osteoarthritis can affect any joint in the body, but it most commonly affects the knees, hips, hands, and spine.

Symptoms of Osteoarthritis

Common symptoms of osteoarthritis may include:

  • Joint pain
  • Stiffness and reduced range of motion
  • Swelling around the joint
  • Tenderness
  • Joint instability
  • Grating sensation (crepitus) when moving the joint

Causes and Risk Factors

Osteoarthritis is primarily a result of aging and wear and tear on the joints over time. However, certain factors may increase the risk of developing osteoarthritis, including:

  • Age
  • Obesity
  • Joint injuries or repetitive stress on joints
  • Family history of osteoarthritis
  • Gender (women are more likely to develop knee osteoarthritis)

Treatment and Management

While there is no cure for osteoarthritis, various treatment options can help manage the symptoms and improve joint function. Treatment may include:

  • Pain relief medications
  • Physical therapy and exercises
  • Weight management
  • Assistive devices
  • Injections (e.g., corticosteroids or hyaluronic acid)
  • In severe cases, joint replacement surgery
