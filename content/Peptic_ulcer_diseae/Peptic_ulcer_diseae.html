Peptic Ulcer Disease Information

What is Peptic Ulcer Disease?

Peptic Ulcer Disease (PUD) refers to open sores that develop on the lining of the stomach, upper small intestine, or esophagus. These sores, known as ulcers, are caused by the erosion of the protective lining due to the stomach acid. Peptic ulcers can be painful and may lead to various complications if left untreated.

Symptoms of Peptic Ulcer Disease

Common symptoms of peptic ulcer disease may include:

  • Burning stomach pain
  • Feeling of fullness or bloating
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Indigestion
  • Unintended weight loss
  • Bloody or dark stool (a sign of bleeding ulcers)

Causes and Risk Factors

Peptic ulcers are often caused by the presence of a bacterium called Helicobacter pylori (H. pylori) or prolonged use of nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs). Risk factors for developing peptic ulcer disease may include:

  • Infection with H. pylori bacteria
  • Regular use of NSAIDs (e.g., aspirin, ibuprofen)
  • Smoking
  • Excessive alcohol consumption
  • Family history of peptic ulcers

Treatment and Management

Treatment for peptic ulcer disease aims to heal the ulcers, relieve symptoms, and prevent complications. Depending on the cause, treatment options may include:

  • Antibiotics to eradicate H. pylori infection
  • Acid-suppressing medications
  • Antacids
  • Proton pump inhibitors (PPIs)
