Pneumonia Information

What is Pneumonia?

Pneumonia is a lung infection that can affect one or both lungs. It can be caused by various pathogens, including bacteria, viruses, and fungi. Pneumonia leads to inflammation of the air sacs in the lungs, causing them to fill with fluid or pus. This can make breathing difficult and lead to a range of symptoms.

Symptoms of Pneumonia

Common symptoms of pneumonia may include:

  • Cough, with or without phlegm
  • Fever and chills
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest pain
  • Fatigue
  • Confusion (in older adults)

Causes and Risk Factors

Pneumonia can be caused by a variety of factors, including:

  • Bacterial infections (e.g., Streptococcus pneumoniae)
  • Viral infections (e.g., influenza virus)
  • Fungal infections
  • Aspiration of food or liquids into the lungs
  • Smoking or exposure to secondhand smoke
  • Age (very young children and older adults are at higher risk)
  • Weak immune system

Treatment and Management

Treatment for pneumonia depends on the cause and severity of the infection. It may include:

  • Antibiotics (for bacterial pneumonia)
  • Antiviral medications (for viral pneumonia)
  • Antifungal medications (for fungal pneumonia)
  • Supportive care, such as rest, hydration, and pain relievers
  • Hospitalization in severe cases
