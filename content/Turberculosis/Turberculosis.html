Tuberculosis (TB) Information

What is Tuberculosis?

Tuberculosis (TB) is an infectious disease caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium tuberculosis. It primarily affects the lungs but can also target other parts of the body. TB spreads through the air when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. It is a significant global health concern, and early detection and treatment are crucial to prevent its spread.

Symptoms of Tuberculosis

Common symptoms of tuberculosis may include:

  • Persistent cough that lasts for weeks
  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Coughing up blood or phlegm
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Unintended weight loss
  • Fever and night sweats
  • Chills

Causes and Risk Factors

Tuberculosis is caused by the Mycobacterium tuberculosis bacteria. Risk factors for developing tuberculosis may include:

  • Close contact with an infected person
  • Living or working in crowded conditions
  • Having a weakened immune system (e.g., HIV/AIDS)
  • Malnutrition
  • Age (young children and older adults are more susceptible)
  • Substance abuse (e.g., alcohol or drug use)

Treatment and Management

Tuberculosis can be treated and cured with a combination of antibiotics. Treatment usually involves a course of multiple drugs taken over several months. It is essential to complete the full course of treatment to prevent the development of drug-resistant TB strains.
