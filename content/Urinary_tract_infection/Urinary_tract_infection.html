Urinary Tract Infection (UTI) Information

What is Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)?

A Urinary Tract Infection (UTI) is an infection that can occur in any part of the urinary system, including the kidneys, ureters, bladder, and urethra. Most UTIs affect the lower urinary tract (bladder and urethra) and are more common in women. UTIs are usually caused by bacteria and can cause discomfort and pain.

Symptoms of Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)

Common symptoms of UTI may include:

  • Strong and persistent urge to urinate
  • Frequent urination
  • Burning sensation during urination
  • Cloudy or bloody urine
  • Feeling tired or shaky
  • Fever or chills (if the infection spreads to the kidneys)

Causes and Risk Factors

UTIs are usually caused by bacteria, most commonly Escherichia coli (E. coli), which can enter the urinary tract through the urethra. Risk factors for developing UTI may include:

  • Female anatomy (shorter urethra, closer to the anus)
  • Sexual activity
  • Use of certain birth control methods (e.g., diaphragm)
  • Menopause
  • Urinary tract abnormalities or blockages

Treatment and Management

UTIs can be treated with antibiotics. It is essential to complete the full course of treatment prescribed by a healthcare provider to ensure the infection is fully cleared. Drinking plenty of water and practicing good hygiene can also help prevent UTIs.
