Varicose Veins Information

What are Varicose Veins?

Varicose veins are enlarged and twisted veins that usually appear blue or dark purple. They commonly occur in the legs and can be seen through the skin's surface. Varicose veins are caused by weakened valves and veins, which can lead to the pooling of blood and the formation of bulging veins. While they are generally harmless, they may cause discomfort and cosmetic concerns.

Symptoms of Varicose Veins

Common symptoms of varicose veins may include:

  • Visible, twisted, and bulging veins
  • Aching or heavy feeling in the legs
  • Swelling in the legs
  • Burning or throbbing sensation
  • Itching around the affected veins
  • Leg cramps
  • Discomfort worsened by prolonged sitting or standing

Causes and Risk Factors

Varicose veins are primarily caused by weak or damaged valves in the veins that hinder the blood flow. Risk factors for developing varicose veins may include:

  • Age (as people get older, veins may lose elasticity)
  • Gender (women are more likely to develop varicose veins)
  • Pregnancy
  • Family history of varicose veins
  • Obesity
  • Standing or sitting for long periods regularly

Treatment and Management

Treatment for varicose veins may include self-care measures, lifestyle changes, and medical interventions. Self-care measures may involve regular exercise, elevating the legs, wearing compression stockings, and avoiding prolonged sitting or standing. Medical treatments may include sclerotherapy, laser treatment, radiofrequency ablation, or surgical procedures.
